BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS & REVENUE MODEL TEMPLATES (Reference for Startup Blueprint Generator)

BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS - 9 BUILDING BLOCKS

1. Customer Segments
- Who are you creating value for? Define primary user/customer groups (e.g. mass market, niche market, segmented, multi-sided platform)

2. Value Proposition
- What problem are you solving, and what unique value do you deliver? Focus on the core benefit that differentiates the offering from alternatives

3. Channels
- How will you reach and deliver value to customers? (e.g. direct sales, app/website, partner distribution, social media, marketplaces)

4. Customer Relationships
- What type of relationship does each customer segment expect? (e.g. self-service, personal assistance, automated services, community-driven)

5. Revenue Streams
- How does the business earn money from each customer segment? (see revenue models below)

6. Key Resources
- What assets are required to make the business model work? (e.g. technology/IP, physical infrastructure, human capital, financial capital)

7. Key Activities
- What are the most important things the business must do to operate? (e.g. product development, platform maintenance, supply chain management, marketing)

8. Key Partnerships
- Who are the suppliers/partners needed? (e.g. technology partners, distribution partners, government bodies, incubators)

9. Cost Structure
- What are the major costs involved? (e.g. fixed costs like salaries/rent, variable costs like customer acquisition, technology infrastructure costs)

COMMON REVENUE MODELS

Subscription Model
- Recurring fee (monthly/annual) for continued access to product/service
- Best for: SaaS, content platforms, membership services

Freemium Model
- Basic features free, premium features paid
- Best for: Apps and platforms aiming for high user acquisition first, monetization later

Marketplace Commission
- Percentage fee on transactions between buyers and sellers on the platform
- Best for: Two-sided marketplaces (e.g. e-commerce, service marketplaces, gig platforms)

Transaction/Usage-Based Fee
- Pay-per-use or pay-per-transaction pricing
- Best for: Payment platforms, API/infrastructure products, logistics

Licensing Model
- Charge other businesses to use your technology, IP, or content
- Best for: B2B tech companies, content creators

Advertising Model
- Revenue from displaying ads to a large user base
- Best for: High-traffic content platforms, media, social apps

Direct Sales / One-Time Purchase
- Single upfront payment for a product
- Best for: Physical products, one-time software licenses

Hybrid Models
- Combining two or more of the above (e.g. freemium + advertising, subscription + marketplace commission)

BUDGET ESTIMATION FRAMEWORK (Reference categories for 6-month runway estimate)

- Product Development: technology/development costs, tools, hosting/cloud infrastructure
- Team/Salaries: founder/core team compensation (even if minimal at early stage)
- Legal & Compliance: registration fees, trademark filing, contracts/agreements
- Marketing & Customer Acquisition: digital marketing, content, launch campaigns
- Operations: office/co-working space, utilities, software subscriptions
- Contingency Buffer: typically 10-15% of total budget for unplanned expenses

GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

1. Target Segment Definition - identify the beachhead market (narrowest viable starting segment)
2. Positioning - what makes the offering distinct in that segment
3. Channel Strategy - primary acquisition channels (organic, paid, partnerships, direct sales)
4. Launch Plan - phased rollout (soft launch/beta, public launch, scale-up)
5. Early Traction Metrics - what to measure in first 90 days (signups, retention, revenue, feedback)

USAGE NOTE FOR AGENT: When generating a blueprint, fill each of the 9 Business Model Canvas blocks specifically for the user's idea (not generically), select the most fitting revenue model(s) based on the product type, and produce a go-to-market plan following the 5-step framework above.
