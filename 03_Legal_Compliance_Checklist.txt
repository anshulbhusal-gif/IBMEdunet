LEGAL & COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS FOR NEW STARTUPS - INDIA (Reference for Startup Blueprint Generator)

1. BUSINESS REGISTRATION TYPES

Sole Proprietorship
- Simplest form, single owner, unlimited personal liability
- Best for: Very small, low-risk businesses, freelancers

Partnership Firm
- Two or more partners, governed by Indian Partnership Act 1932
- Unlimited liability unless registered as LLP

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
- Combines partnership flexibility with limited liability protection
- Good for: Professional services, small teams wanting liability protection without heavy compliance

Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd)
- Most common structure for startups seeking investment
- Limited liability, separate legal entity, easier to raise VC funding
- Requires minimum 2 directors, 2 shareholders, registered office
- Governed by Companies Act 2013, registered via MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs)

One Person Company (OPC)
- Single founder wants limited liability and company structure
- Fewer compliance requirements than Pvt Ltd but has limits on further fundraising flexibility

2. CORE REGISTRATIONS AND COMPLIANCE STEPS

- PAN and TAN registration for the business entity
- GST Registration: mandatory if annual turnover exceeds threshold (currently INR 20 lakh for services, INR 40 lakh for goods, varies by state); voluntary registration also possible for input credit benefits
- Udyam Registration (MSME): free registration for micro/small/medium enterprise benefits
- DPIIT Startup Recognition: apply via Startup India portal for tax and compliance benefits
- Shops and Establishment Act registration (state-specific, required for physical business premises)
- Professional Tax registration (state-specific, applicable in Maharashtra and other states)

3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY BASICS

- Trademark registration: protects brand name/logo, filed with Indian Trademark Registry, DPIIT-recognized startups get 50% rebate on filing fees
- Patent: for novel inventions/processes, DPIIT-recognized startups get 80% rebate on filing fees and fast-track examination
- Copyright: automatically applies to original creative/software work but formal registration strengthens legal standing
- Trade secrets: protect via confidentiality agreements (NDAs) with employees, contractors, partners

4. EMPLOYMENT AND LABOR COMPLIANCE

- Employment agreements/offer letters with clear terms
- Provident Fund (PF) and Employee State Insurance (ESI) registration once employee count crosses statutory thresholds
- DPIIT-recognized startups can self-certify compliance with 6 labor laws and 3 environmental laws for 3-5 years, reducing inspection burden

5. DATA PROTECTION AND SECTOR-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE

- Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) compliance if handling user personal data
- Sector-specific licenses may apply: FSSAI for food businesses, RBI/NBFC registration for fintech lending, drug license for healthtech/pharma

6. FOUNDER AGREEMENTS

- Founders' agreement covering equity split, vesting schedule, roles, exit clauses - strongly recommended before incorporation
- Vesting typically over 4 years with a 1-year cliff to protect against early co-founder departure

USAGE NOTE FOR AGENT: Recommend Pvt Ltd as default structure when the user plans to raise external funding; recommend LLP or OPC for smaller, self-funded, low-fundraising-intent ventures. Always flag GST, DPIIT recognition, and founders' agreement as near-universal first steps regardless of sector.
